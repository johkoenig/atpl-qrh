# 070 Operational Procecdures

## 01 - General Requirements

### ICAO Annex 6 Applicability

- Operators authorized to conduct international CAT.
- Safety Management, if aircraft > 27t are used

### Management

- Accountable Manager: Ultimate responsibility and authority
- Safety Manager: Manages SMS and day-to-day operation

### Maintenance

- Written maintenance release must be signed by the engineering staff after work
- Pilot then counter-signs to accept the aircraft back to service

### Flight Safety Documents System

- System holding safety and operational information
- Relevant for all operational crew (not just pilots)

### Lights at night

- 3 navigation lights (red, green, white)
- 2 landing lights

### Min. Landing distance

- Turbojet: 0.6 (Factor 1.66)
- Turboprop: 0.7 (Factor 1.43)

### Inexperienced pilot

- Less than 100 h and 10 sectors within 120 days
- Less than 150 h and 20 sectors (no time limit)

### Contaminated runways

- Surface conditions:
  - Dry
  - Damp: Moisture with shiny appearance
  - Wet: Moisture appears reflective
  - Contaminated: Ice, Water/slush/snow > 3 mm, Compacted snow (all are falling from cloud)
- Max. speed for hydroplaning:
  - Rotating tyres: v > 9 * sqrt(tyre press in psi)
  - Non-rotating tyres: v > 7.7 * sqrt(tyre press in psi)
- Hydroplaning types:
  - Dynamic: On standing water
  - Viscous: On water + dirt or rubber
  - Reverted rubber: blocked tyre, heating up, forming steam bubble
